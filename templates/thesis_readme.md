## Quick Start: Refining in Cursor

1. **Open this folder in Cursor**
   ```bash
   cursor your_thesis/
   ```

2. **Use the built-in prompts** in `tools/`:
   - `humanizer_prompt.md` - Match your writing style
   - `entropy_prompt.md` - Increase linguistic diversity
   - `style_guide.md` - APA 7th edition reference

3. **The `.cursorrules` file** automatically configures Cursor AI for thesis refinement

## Refinement Workflow

### Step 1: Review the Research
Start by reading through `research/papers/` to understand what sources informed your thesis.

### Step 2: Edit Section Drafts
Open files in `drafts/` and refine each section:
- Use Cursor's AI with the prompts in `tools/`
- Reference `research/papers/` for citation accuracy

### Step 3: Apply Humanizer
Use `tools/humanizer_prompt.md` to match your natural writing style.

### Step 4: Check AI Detection
Use `tools/entropy_prompt.md` to ensure natural-sounding prose.

### Step 5: Re-export
After edits, regenerate PDF/DOCX from `exports/FINAL_THESIS.md`.

## Tips

- **Keep citations intact** - Never remove citation IDs like `{cite_001}`
- **Reference your research** - The `papers/` folder is your source of truth
- **Use Cursor's context** - The `.cursorrules` understands thesis structure
- **Iterate** - Run humanizer/entropy multiple times for best results
